Interest in IT

The first spark of interest in I.T began when I was around eight years old, with my older sister playing The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time on the Nintendo 64. I would spend hours watching her play and would be completely enraptured by the game and the technology behind it. My interest in technology in general then grew from there. I would play games on the chunky family computer and would tinker with programs like Microsoft Paint or Publisher. My interest in programs continued to grow as I got older and I found video games and creative programs was what I was most drawn to. I realised more recently through a programming game on my Nintendo Switch that I.T was a subject I wanted to have more knowledge on and I wanted to start pursuing as a career.

When I first realised I wanted to study I.T I found myself at a standstill. Working full time in Accounts, I felt like I had missed my opportunity to grow and change careers as there was no way I could attend university and continue to support myself financially. That’s when I came across online university options and this led me to RMIT. I had heard good things about RMIT from a few family members that are based in Melbourne and so I became confident that this was a university that was going to offer me the education I was looking for in the best way to suit me.

During my studies at RMIT, I am hoping to learn a lot more about the fundamentals of I.T. My current knowledge base is very limited and I am excited to see how much more there is to learn about. Even from viewing the subjects that are needed to complete the Bachelor of Information Technology, I can already see that there are so many things I wasn’t aware of that are involved in I.T.

Project Idea

Overview

This project would be a mobile application that combines both responsibility and recreation. The application would be a tracker of your savings combined with a game. Each time you put money towards your saving goals or each time you hit a certain milestone, you would be rewarded within the game, whether it helps you progress further into the game or rewards you with an item etc.

Motivation

From a young age, many people within the world get their first job and start saving money. In Australia alone, the average age of first employment is 16 years old. However, there are still a lot of people who struggle to save money.

With this application, the aim would be to help motivate people to save money in a fun and enjoyable manner, while still keeping close track of their money in and out. Instead of it being an arduous task with messy tracking of information, the application would make saving fun and enjoyable, while still keeping a close eye on your daily finances.

With an average of 89% of Australians using smartphones daily, this application would appeal to a large market of users who struggle to keep up with saving goals.
